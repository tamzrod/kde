## Analysis

### KDE-001 (Knowledge)

**Assessment**: MIXED

The "actionable understanding" criterion works differently in creative domain:
- In engineering: action = practical work
- In creative: action = creative decisions enabling further work

The criterion is applicable but requires domain interpretation.

### KDE-002 (Evidence)

**Assessment**: SUPPORTS

Evidence in creative domain differs from engineering:
- Engineering: logs, code, specs, measurements
- Creative: genre conventions, decisions, established facts

Evidence types differ but traceability principle remains valid.

### KDE-003 (Ambiguity)

**Assessment**: MIXED

The definition identifies ambiguity but does not resolve it:
- In engineering: resolution through technical criteria
- In creative: resolution through creative choice

Both domains experience "multiplicity of interpretation" but resolve differently.

## Cross-Domain Observations

### Methodology Observations

1. **Lifecycle applies**: HYPOTHESIS → DESIGN → EXECUTION → OBSERVATION → EVIDENCE → CONCLUSION → ASSESSMENT works

2. **Traceability valid**: Every creative decision can be traced to evidence (genre knowledge, prior decisions)

3. **Ambiguity classification works**: Blocking/productive distinction applies to creative decisions

4. **Reproducibility differs**: Engineering reproducibility = consistent results; Creative reproducibility = consistent methodology, not results

5. **Evidence types differ**: Creative "evidence" includes genre conventions, established facts, story structure principles

### Domain Challenges

1. **Actionability interpretation**: "Actionable" requires creative interpretation

2. **Reproducibility definition**: Engineering reproducibility differs from creative reproducibility

3. **Truth vs utility**: Engineering seeks truth; creative seeks utility (engaging story)

4. **Multiple valid solutions**: Engineering often has optimal solution; creative has multiple valid solutions

## Conclusion

The Laboratory methodology can be applied to creative domain with the following observations:

**Works Well**:
- Lifecycle structure
- Traceability requirements
- Ambiguity identification
- Evidence documentation

**Requires Interpretation**:
- "Actionable" criterion (creative interpretation)
- "Reproducibility" definition (methodology, not results)
- "Evidence" types (genre knowledge, decisions)

**Limitations**:
- Does not resolve ambiguity (requires creative choice)
- Cannot determine "optimal" creative solution
- Reproducibility means consistent process, not identical results
