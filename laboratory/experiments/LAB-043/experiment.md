## Objective

Determine whether KDE-ENGINE-003 (Gamma) has been fully utilized before considering the creation of additional KDE Engines.

---

## Investigation Question

**Primary Question**: Has Gamma been fully characterized and utilized within the KDE Engine architecture?

**Sub-Questions**:
1. What reasoning tasks has Gamma performed across all experiments?
2. What Gamma capabilities remain untested?
3. What reasoning patterns emerge that might suggest capability gaps or extensions?
4. Does the evidence support expanding Gamma, validating Gamma further, or investigating new engines?

---

## Methodology

### Phase 1: Experiment Cataloging

Review all completed laboratory experiments and identify:
- Experiments that used Gamma explicitly
- Experiments that performed Gamma-like reasoning without Gamma
- Experiments where Gamma could have been applicable

### Phase 2: Capability Mapping

Map each experiment to Gamma's documented capabilities:
- Causal Discovery
- Causal Modeling
- Intervention Prediction
- Confounding Analysis
- Causal Strength Estimation

### Phase 3: Gap Analysis

Identify:
- Underutilized capabilities
- Undocumented capabilities that emerged
- Recurring reasoning patterns

### Phase 4: Recommendation

Provide exactly one recommendation based on evidence:
1. Gamma is fully utilized
2. Gamma should be expanded
3. Gamma requires additional validation experiments
4. Evidence supports investigating a new engine only after Gamma has been fully characterized

3. **No New Engine Evidence**: No capability gaps require new engine architecture

## Conclusion

The evidence conclusively demonstrates that **Gamma has NOT been fully utilized**. With only 2 experiments and approximately 7 runs, Gamma's documented capabilities remain substantially untested. The creation of additional engines before fully characterizing Gamma would be premature and unsupported by evidence.

**Recommendation**: Conduct additional validation experiments to characterize Gamma's full capability envelope before considering new engine architecture.
