# Conclusions: LAB-013 - Cross-Domain Applicability

**Experiment**: LAB-013
**Date**: 2026-07-19
**Status**: COMPLETE

---

## Executive Summary

LAB-013 determined the applicability boundaries of the Information DNA methodology across 6 diverse domains. The experiment identified a stable core that applies universally and domain-specific adaptations that require calibration.

**Result**: The methodology has a **stable core** that applies universally and **adaptable components** that require domain-specific handling.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Stable Core Identified

The following components remain valid across all tested domains:

| Component | Evidence | Confidence |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| Traceability principle | 100% across all domains | HIGH |
| Evidence-based nature | All domains | HIGH |
| 9-field DNA structure | All experiments | HIGH |
| Ambiguity identification | All domains | MEDIUM |
| Observation-evidence linking | 100% coverage | HIGH |

### 2. Domain Adaptations Required

| Component | Adaptation Required | Domains |
|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| Confidence thresholds | YES | All domains |
| Reproducibility definition | YES | Creative, Legal |
| Evidence type taxonomy | YES | All domains |
| Ambiguity resolution | YES | All domains |

### 3. Applicability Boundary Determined

**Full Applicability (>60%)**: Mathematics, Engineering (Standards), Scientific Research, Software Engineering, Creative Writing

**Partial Applicability (30-60%)**: Legal

**Outside Applicability (Unknown)**: Intuitive, spiritual, undocumented, unverifiable knowledge

## Applicability Boundary

### SYN-BOUND-003: Applicability Boundary

The methodology applies fully to formal domains (evidence verifiable, reproducibility achievable). Applicability degrades in interpretive domains where contradictions are inherent and reproducibility is not achievable.

**Evidence**: OBS-066, OBS-067, OBS-068 (RUN-007)
**Confidence**: HIGH
