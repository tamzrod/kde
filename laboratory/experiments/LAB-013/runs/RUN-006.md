## Theoretical Analysis

Legal represents a domain with formal structure but high interpretation requirements:

### Strengths
1. **Formal sources**: Statutes, regulations, precedents
2. **Evidence hierarchy**: Court rulings, legal documents
3. **Structure**: Clear citation systems, precedents
4. **Accountability**: Legal reasoning is documented

### Challenges
1. **Interpretation**: Same law, different rulings possible
2. **Precedent**: Contradictory precedents exist
3. **Change**: Laws change over time
4. **Context**: Same law applies differently by jurisdiction

## Critical Finding

**OBS-055**: Contradictory precedents are a feature, not a bug

Unlike other domains where contradictions indicate failure:
- Legal: Contradictory precedents require interpretation
- Creative: Contradicting world facts create tension
- Engineering: Contradictions indicate error

This suggests **KDE-003** (ambiguity) may be fundamentally different in legal.

**OBS-053**: Legal reproducibility ≠ Scientific reproducibility

Same evidence (law) + different judges = different ruling
Same evidence (experiment) + different scientists = same result

This is a fundamental difference in reproducibility definition.
