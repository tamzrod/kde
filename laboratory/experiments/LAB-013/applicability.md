## Applicable Components

### Stable Core (Universal)

These components apply universally across all domains:

1. **Traceability Principle**
   - Every observation must have evidence
   - Every evidence must support observation
   - **Evidence**: OBS-062 (100% across all domains)

2. **Evidence-Based Nature**
   - All conclusions must be evidence-backed
   - **Evidence**: OBS-061 (all domains)

3. **9-Field DNA Structure**
   - DNA ID, Canonical Agreement, Supporting Observations, etc.
   - **Evidence**: All experiments

4. **Ambiguity Identification**
   - Ambiguity exists in all domains
   - Classification (blocking/productive) applies
   - **Evidence**: OBS-065

5. **Observation-Evidence Linking**
   - Systematic linking required
   - **Evidence**: OBS-062

### Domain-Adaptive Components

These components require calibration per domain:

1. **Confidence Thresholds**
   - Engineering: 5-6 runs → HIGH
   - Software: 10 runs → MEDIUM
   - Creative: 10+ runs → MEDIUM

2. **Reproducibility Definition**
   - Formal domains: Consistent results
   - Creative domains: Consistent methodology
   - Legal: Case-by-case basis

3. **Evidence Type Taxonomy**
   - Formal: Standards, proofs
   - Empirical: Observations, experiments
   - Interpretive: Conventions, decisions

4. **Ambiguity Resolution**
   - Formal: Through technical criteria
   - Creative: Through creative choice
   - Legal: Through legal argumentation

## Applicability Boundaries

### Full Applicability Boundary (>60%)

**Domains**: Mathematics, Engineering, Science, Software, Creative

**Criteria**:
- Evidence is verifiable (internally or externally)
- Reproducibility achievable (results or methodology)
- Ambiguity resolvable (technical or creative criteria)

### Partial Applicability Boundary (30-60%)

**Domains**: Legal (as tested)

**Criteria**:
- Evidence is traceable but interpretation varies
- Reproducibility limited (case-by-case)
- Ambiguity inherent (by design, not error)

### Outside Applicability (Unknown)

**Potential domains not tested**:
- Intuitive knowledge
- Spiritual/philosophical knowledge
- Undocumented practices
- Unverifiable claims

## Conclusion

**Applicability**: The Information DNA methodology applies universally but with varying degrees of success.

**Stable Core**: Traceability, evidence-based nature, 9-field structure, ambiguity identification, observation-evidence linking.

**Adaptations Required**: Confidence thresholds, reproducibility definition, evidence taxonomy, ambiguity resolution approach.

**Boundary**: Full applicability in formal domains; partial applicability in interpretive domains where contradictions are inherent and reproducibility is not achievable.
