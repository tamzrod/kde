## Narrative Arc

### Book 1: The Prometheus Circuit (2247-2249)

**Premise**: First generation of Mars-born children reach adulthood. Competition for resources between Earth-Luna Hegemony and Martian Commonwealth threatens to become violent.

**Protagonist**: Maya Chen-Reyes, born on Mars, former NTR pilot for the Martian Freight Authority, now working as a salvage operator in the Belt.

**Key Events**:
1. Maya discovers derelict alien probe near Ceres
2. Evidence of intelligent non-human life in the outer system
3. Political tensions escalate between Earth and Mars
4. Maya recruited by the Interplanetary Science Directorate
5. Race to reach the probe before military forces claim it

### Book 2: The Venus Gambit (2250-2253)

**Premise**: With first alien contact imminent, Earth and Mars compete to establish diplomatic priority. Maya leads a dangerous mission to Venus's upper atmosphere where alien signals originate.

**New Characters**:
- Dr. Yusuf Okonkwo - linguist tasked with first contact
- Admiral Sarah Volkov - Earth Fleet commander
- Jin Reyes - Maya's estranged brother, Mars military intelligence

### Book 3: The Long Dark (2254-2261)

**Premise**: After first contact goes wrong, humanity must unite against a common threat. Seven-year time jump as Maya leads a coalition fleet on the longest voyage ever attempted.

## Scientific Accuracy Checklist

### Propulsion
- [ ] No reactionless drives (EmDrive, Alcubierre in FTl sections)
- [ ] Delta-v budgets calculated for major maneuvers
- [ ] Travel times consistent with acceleration limits (1-3g max for crews)
- [ ] Fuel fractions realistic for mission profiles

### Orbital Mechanics
- [ ] Hohmann transfers used for standard transit
- [ ] Gravity assists mentioned where applicable
- [ ] Orbital periods and distances accurate
- [ ] Lagrange points utilized for station locations

### Environmental
- [ ] Vacuum properties described correctly (no sound, rapid cooling, pressure)
- [ ] Radiation hazards addressed (solar flares, cosmic rays)
- [ ] Microgravity effects on humans (bone loss, fluid shift)
- [ ] Heat management in space (radiators, shading)

### Technology
- [ ] Artificial gravity via rotation (centripetal)
- [ ] Closed-loop life support (air recycling, water reclamation)
- [ ] In-situ resource utilization (ice mining, atmospheric processing)
- [ ] Communication delays across distances
