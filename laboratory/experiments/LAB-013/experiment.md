## Objective

Determine the applicability boundaries of the Information DNA methodology across diverse knowledge domains.

## Research Question

Under what conditions does the Information DNA methodology remain valid?

## Background

LAB-011 demonstrated that Information DNA synthesis can produce deterministic, traceable knowledge structures.

LAB-012 independently validated the methodology and identified important limitations:
- Domain-dependent confidence
- Varying definitions of reproducibility
- Possible validity boundaries

LAB-013 investigates those boundaries.

## Hypothesis

The Information DNA methodology contains:
1. A stable core that is domain independent
2. Domain-specific behaviors that require calibration

## Domain Selection

The following domains will be analyzed based on existing experiments:

| Engineering (Standards) | LAB-006 | Formal standards, verifiable, RFCs |

| Mathematics | External analysis | Axioms, proofs, formal verification |
| Legal | External analysis | Precedent, statutes, interpretation |

## Methodology

### Phase 1: Domain Analysis
For each domain, analyze:
- Knowledge structure
- Evidence quality
- Reproducibility characteristics
- Ambiguity types
- Interpretation requirements

### Phase 2: Synthesis Measurement
For each domain, measure:
- Observation quality
- Agreement stability
- DNA determinism
- Traceability
- Relationship consistency
- Evidence quality

### Phase 3: Comparison
Across domains, determine:
- What remains invariant
- What changes
- What fails
- What requires calibration

### Phase 4: Falsification
Search for:
- Failure cases
- Edge cases
- Impossible syntheses
- Broken relationships
