# LinkedIn Best Practices Research

**Experiment**: LAB-030
**Type**: Research
**Date**: 2026-07-22

---

## Research Questions

1. What LinkedIn best practices apply to technical/engineering content?
2. What hooks and formats drive highest engagement?
3. What timing and posting strategy optimizes reach?
4. What metrics should be tracked?

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Format Performance

| Format | Engagement Rate | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Native documents/carousels | ~7% | Highest saves/shares |
| Image posts | 3-5% | Good for visual content |
| Text posts | 2-4% | Depends heavily on hook |
| Video | 4-6% | Completion rate matters |
| External links | Penalized | Reduces early distribution |

**Source**: Social Media Today, SocialInsider

### Finding 2: Hook Effectiveness

- Hooks <140 characters before "see more" truncation
- Contrarian hooks generate 3x more comments
- Quantified promises outperform vague claims
- Pattern-interrupt style most effective

**Source**: PressMaster, AuthoredUp

### Finding 3: Algorithm Behavior

- LinkedIn shifted to interest-based ranking (2024-2025)
- Early engagement (first 60-90 min) critical for distribution
- Dwell time > likes for algorithm weighting
- Saves indicate high-value content
- Anti-spam measures penalize mass hashtags/links

**Source**: SocialBee, TeraCT AI, LinkedIn Official

### Finding 4: Posting Strategy

- Optimal days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- Optimal time: 7-9 AM EST / 12-2 PM EST
- Optimal frequency: 3-5 posts/week for personal profiles
- First 60 minutes = "golden hour" for engagement

**Source**: Hashmeta, Inspired Marketing B2B

## Audience-Specific Findings

### AI/ML Engineers
- Value reproducible artifacts (code, configs)
- Respond to benchmark/metric-heavy content
- Prefer technical depth over marketing speak

### Engineering Leaders
- Respond to process/predictability messaging
- Value org-level outcomes
- Interested in team efficiency

### Research Engineers
- Value methodology rigor
- Interested in novel approaches
- Appreciate evidence-based claims

## Synthesis

The research converges on several principles:

1. **Hook is king** - Strong opening determines distribution
2. **Native > external** - In-platform content preferred
3. **Quality > quantity** - Engagement signals matter more than likes
4. **Timing matters** - First hour critical
5. **Consistency counts** - Regular posting builds momentum
