## Post 3: Methodology Deep-Dive (Week 3)

Use this post to establish technical credibility:

**The 5 core principles that make knowledge validation actually work.**

After 29 experiments, we discovered something counterintuitive:

Knowledge validation isn't about finding "right" answers.

It's about creating systems that catch wrong ones.

Here's what we learned:

**1. Evidence Over Authority**
Don't accept knowledge because an expert said so.
Accept it because evidence supports it.

**2. Humans Approve; AI Recommends**
AI can surface patterns.
Humans must approve conclusions.
No self-certification.

**3. Continuous Testing**
One validation isn't enough.
Knowledge must be continuously tested.

**4. Explicit Uncertainty**
Label what is evidence vs. inference vs. hypothesis.
Don't let readers guess.

**5. Reproducibility Required**
If it can't be reproduced, it's not validated.
It's just asserted.

These principles sound obvious.

But most teams skip them when under pressure.
