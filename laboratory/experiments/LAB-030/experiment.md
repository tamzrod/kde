## Objective

Determine the most effective evidence-based communication strategy for introducing KDE to the engineering and AI community through LinkedIn.

---

## Research Question

What communication approach will most effectively introduce KDE to technical audiences on LinkedIn while maintaining scientific credibility?

---

## Methodology

1. KDE self-assessment (philosophy, principles, differentiators)
2. LinkedIn best practices research (2024-2025 algorithm, formats, timing)
3. Target audience analysis (AI/ML engineers, engineering leaders)
4. Hypothesis generation (3 candidate approaches)
5. Comparative evaluation (evidence-based scoring)
6. Final recommendation

## Experiment Summary

This experiment produced three candidate LinkedIn posts:

- **Candidate A**: "Problem First" - Relatable pain point approach
- **Candidate B**: "Contrarian" - Challenges assumptions (RECOMMENDED)
- **Candidate C**: "Story" - Walks through concrete validation example

Recommendation: **Candidate B** (Contrarian Hook) based on:
- Evidence of 3x engagement increase with pattern-interrupt hooks
- Strong first-impression potential
- Aligns with KDE's evidence-over-authority principle
