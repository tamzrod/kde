## 1. Research Summary

### 1.1 KDE Self-Assessment

#### What KDE Truly Is

**KDE (Knowledge Discovery Engine)** is a research framework for discovering and validating knowledge through evidence-based scientific methodology. It is NOT:
- An AI tool or product
- A knowledge base
- A publishing platform
- A framework du jour

KDE is a **meta-framework** — a system for rigorously validating how knowledge becomes accepted as true.

#### What Makes KDE Different

| Attribute | KDE Approach | Conventional Approach |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Validation** | Evidence before acceptance | Authority-based acceptance |
| **Governance** | Humans approve; AI recommends | Self-certification |
| **Immutability** | Frozen seeds preserve reasoning | Mutable revisions |
| **Iteration** | Scientific loop continuous | One-time validation |
| **Domain** | Domain-independent | Domain-specific |

#### Core Principles (From SEED-001)

1. **No Auto-Continuation** — AI waits for human authorization
2. **No Self-Approval** — Only humans approve work
3. **No Self-Promotion** — Only humans promote knowledge
4. **Distinguish Evidence/Inference/Hypothesis** — Explicit labeling
5. **Evidence-Based Changes** — All claims justified by evidence

#### What People Are Likely to Misunderstand

| Misunderstanding | Reality |
|------------------|---------|
| "Another AI framework" | A methodology framework for validation |
| "Generates knowledge" | Validates knowledge through experiments |
| "Like LangChain/LlamaIndex" | Not a tool, but a governance structure |
| "Research paper" | Living methodology with 29+ experiments |
| "For academics only" | Domain-independent engineering framework |

#### What Should Be Communicated First

- **Hook**: A compelling problem KDE solves (false knowledge spreading)
- **Core message**: Evidence-based knowledge validation
- **Differentiation**: Why rigor matters for AI/engineering

#### What Should NOT Be Communicated Yet

- Full architecture details (seeds, engines)
- 29+ experiments catalog
- Governance complexity
- Speculative capabilities

## 11. Experiment Conclusions

### 11.1 Key Findings

#### Finding 1: First-Impression Matters Most

**Evidence**: LinkedIn's algorithm favors early engagement signals (first 60-90 minutes) [SocialBee](https://www.socialbee.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm)

**Implication**: Hook strength is the single most important factor for reach

#### Finding 2: Pattern-Interrupt Outperforms Standard Hooks

**Evidence**: Research shows contrarian and curiosity-based hooks generate 3x more engagement [PressMaster](https://pressmaster.ai/article/linkedin-hook-secrets-that-triple-engagement-rates)

**Implication**: KDE's contrarian stance ("knowledge you trust is probably wrong") is strategically sound

#### Finding 3: Technical Audiences Value Specificity

**Evidence**: B2B engineering audiences respond to metrics, code snippets, and reproducible examples [LinkedIn Algorithm Studies](https://www.linkedin.com/top-content/marketing/linkedin-content-and-ads/trends-in-linkedin-post-length)

**Implication**: Future posts should include specific experiments and evidence

#### Finding 4: Native Content > External Links

**Evidence**: LinkedIn penalizes posts with external links in early distribution [Algorithm Analysis](https://www.teract.ai/resources/linkedin-algorithm-2026)

**Implication**: Drive traffic through comments, not links

### 11.2 Strategic Recommendations

1. **Lead with Problem**: Open with relatable pain point
2. **Establish Credibility**: Use specific numbers (29 experiments)
3. **Be Provocative**: Challenge assumptions, but deliver on promise
4. **Engage Rapidly**: Respond within first hour
5. **Plan Journey**: Single post is insufficient; design multi-post arc
