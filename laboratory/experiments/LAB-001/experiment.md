## Objective

Apply the Tier 1 knowledge framework (KDE-001, KDE-002, KDE-003) to ten consecutive real engineering decisions. Observe where the framework succeeds, fails, or becomes insufficient.

## Knowledge Under Test

| Knowledge ID | Definition | Aspect Tested |
|-------------|------------|----------------|
| KDE-001 | Engineering knowledge is actionable understanding that enables effective practice within constraints. | Action-enablement, constraint recognition |
| KDE-002 | Engineering evidence is retrievable support, in context, that enables evaluation of knowledge claims. | Evidence identification, retrieval |
| KDE-003 | Engineering ambiguity is multiplicity of interpretation that blocks or complicates knowledge use. | Ambiguity identification, classification |

## Hypothesis

The Tier 1 knowledge framework provides sufficient structure to guide real engineering decisions while revealing specific gaps requiring future research.

## Scope

- Maximum 10 decision loops
- Real engineering work only
- No invented scenarios
- No KDE modifications during experiment
