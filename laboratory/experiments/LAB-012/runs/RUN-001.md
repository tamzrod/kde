## Reasoning Summary

### Control Theory Analysis

**H1 - Control Arbiter**: In control theory, a supervisor/arbiter is required when multiple controllers access shared resources. The PPC must arbitrate between AP, RP, and Voltage controllers accessing shared plant equipment. Without arbiter, simultaneous conflicting commands result in undefined plant behavior.

**H2 - Exclusive Control Groups**: Mutual exclusion is fundamental. Free Production vs MW Constraint vs Gradient Override simultaneous activation creates contradictory control objectives (maximize vs limit vs rate-limit), causing oscillatory or unstable behavior.

**H3 - Feedback Validation**: Closed-loop control theory mandates feedback verification. Without measurement confirmation, command could fail silently, leaving plant in unintended state.

**H4 - Protection Override**: Safety hierarchy is inviolable. Protection systems must have highest priority (IEC 61508 SIL-3/SIL-4). Frequency > 61 Hz requires immediate autonomous action.

**H5-H7**: Single owner, exclusivity, and state machines align with controllability and observability requirements.

**H10**: Hierarchical architecture provides modularity but adds latency. 2-second response requirement may be challenging with multiple layers.

**H20**: 5-minute stability may be excessive for electrical dynamics (settling typically seconds) but reasonable for thermal dynamics.
