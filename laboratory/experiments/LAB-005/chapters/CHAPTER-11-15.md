# Chapters 11-15: Revelations

---

## Chapter Summaries

### Chapter 11: The Library

Kael discovers ancient texts in the Academy library describing "World Walkers"—beings who could move between realities. The texts are incomplete and heavily redacted.

**Key revelation**: World Walker theory exists but has been suppressed.

### Chapter 12: The Investigation

Master Elara reveals that the Archmage Council has been suppressing knowledge of World Walkers for centuries. Their existence threatens the mana-based social order.

**Key revelation**: Political dimension to mana knowledge suppression.

### Chapter 13: The Rival's Secret

Varen Stormwind approaches Kael with a secret: he also has an unusual Core—though his is the opposite. He has no mana at all, yet can nullify any magical effect.

**Key revelation**: New character concept introduced. Nullification Core.

### Chapter 14: The Alliance

Kael and Varen form an uneasy alliance. Together, they begin investigating the Archmage Council's activities.

**Key revelation**: Rival becomes ally. Information network develops.

### Chapter 15: The Council

The Archmage Council summons Kael to the capital. They offer him a choice: join them and suppress his true nature, or be declared an enemy of the order.

**Key revelation**: Direct conflict with established power structure.

### Methodology Observation

**OBS-METH-08**: The introduction of World Walkers (WF-063-WF-065) creates a potential connection to the protagonist's origin (WF-007, WF-012). However, the connection is implied, not confirmed. The Laboratory correctly maintains ambiguity (A-026).

**OBS-METH-09**: The antagonist introduction (Archmage Council) follows narrative convention. The methodology does not address how to handle "villain" characters who claim authority but may be acting in self-interest.

**POTENTIAL ISSUE**: Character claims (Elara's claims about Council, Council's claims about World Walkers) create evidentiary challenges. The methodology provides no mechanism for distinguishing between "facts stated by characters" and "demonstrated facts."
