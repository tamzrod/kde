# Chapter 1: The Academy Entrance

---

## Chapter Content

The morning sun cast long shadows across the stone courtyard as fifteen-year-old Kael adjusted his academy robes for the third time. His fingers trembled—not from cold, but from the Mana Core examination that awaited him inside.

The Thornwood Academy of Magical Arts loomed before him, its towers piercing the morning mist like crystallized lightning. Students from noble families lined up in their finest silks, their polished boots clicking against the cobblestones. Kael's secondhand robes marked him as something else.

"Mind your Core, commoner," sneered a boy in crimson velvet—the Varens, if Kael remembered correctly, a mid-tier noble house. "Some Cores simply aren't worth the examination fee."

Kael said nothing. His Analytical Mind had already processed seventeen different mana flow patterns from the examinees around him. The distributions were... predictable. Most followed a Gaussian curve centered on what his previous life's medical knowledge would call "average."

But there was something else. A pattern in the mana that his mind recognized from his previous world: fractal resonance. The magical energy didn't just flow—it oscillated.

"Kael Thornwood," called the examiner at the door.

He walked forward.

The examination chamber was circular, lined with crystallized mana conduits that hummed with barely contained power. Three examiners sat behind a semicircular desk, their robes marking them as Academy Masters.

"Place your hand on the Core Reader," instructed the central examiner, a severe woman whose nameplate read MASTER ELARA.

Kael complied. The moment his palm touched the smooth crystal surface, his Analytical Mind erupted into overdrive.

The Core Reader didn't just measure mana—it measured *potential*. And Kael's potential was unlike anything the machine had been calibrated to detect. His Analytical Mind wasn't just processing the magical formulas in real-time; it was *seeing through the machine's measurement methodology*.

"Interesting," murmured Master Elara, watching the readings flicker. "Your Core grade is... inconsistent. The machine cannot determine a classification."

"I can," Kael said quietly. "My Core grade is Omega."

The room went silent.

Omega-grade Cores were theoretical. Legends. The highest possible classification implied a Core that could theoretically master every magical discipline. They hadn't existed in recorded history.

"You're either a genius or a fraud," Master Elara said. "We'll find out which."
