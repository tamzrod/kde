# Chapter 3: The First Lesson

---

## Chapter Content

Professor Aldric's Introduction to Magical Theory class met in a circular lecture hall beneath the Academy's main tower. The walls were lined with preserved mana crystals that pulsed with soft blue light, and at the center of the room stood a raised platform where the professor would demonstrate magical principles.

Kael arrived early, choosing a seat in the back corner where his Analytical Mind could observe without distraction. Around him, students whispered about the "Unclassified" student, their curiosity poorly disguised as disinterest.

"Today," Professor Aldric began, his voice carrying effortlessly through the hall, "we discuss the fundamental nature of mana."

He raised his hand, and a sphere of crystallized mana materialized above his palm. "Mana is not energy—not in the conventional sense. It is *potential*. The universe's capacity to become something other than what it currently is."

Kael's Analytical Mind latched onto the phrasing. "Capacity to become"—that was remarkably similar to the inaccessible reserves that determined Core grades.

"Professor," Kael said, raising his hand, "if mana is potential, what determines how much potential a Core can access?"

An older student snickered. "That's literally the definition of Core grade. Were you not paying attention at examination?"

Professor Aldric raised a silencing hand. "A fair question, young Thornwood. The answer is: we don't know."

The room went quiet.

"What we know," the professor continued, "is that Cores develop based on three factors: inheritance, environment, and unknown variables. We can measure a Core's *current* output. We cannot predict its *ultimate* potential."

Kael's Analytical Mind began processing. If Core Readers measured output, and output was only part of the accessible mana, then the machines were missing the critical variable: the inaccessible reserves.

The Omega grade wasn't theoretical because Omega Cores were impossible. It was theoretical because no one had ever detected the inaccessible reserves that would prove such Cores existed.

"My question," Kael said, "is whether anyone has ever tried to measure what the Core Reader *cannot* measure."

Professor Aldric smiled—the first genuine smile Kael had seen from an Academy faculty member. "That, Mr. Thornwood, is exactly the right question."
