# Chapter 2: The Omega Lie

---

## Chapter Content

The examination results were posted three days later, pinned to the Academy's central notice board beneath a banner of Thornwood's sigil: a tree struck by lightning.

Kael pushed through the crowd of anxious students, his Analytical Mind already parsing the mana signatures of those around him. He could tell from their Core grades that most of them would be admitted. The Thornwood Academy accepted anyone with a Grade C Core or above.

"Kael Thornwood," read the proctor, not bothering to look up. "Provisional Admission. Grade: UNCLASSIFIED."

Murmurs rippled through the crowd. Unclassified wasn't a grade—it was a designation for those whose Cores couldn't be measured.

"Is there a mistake?" Kael asked. "I tested as Omega."

"You tested as *inconsistent*," Master Elara's voice cut through the noise. She stood beside the proctor, her expression carved from stone. "Omega-grade Cores don't exist. They never have. Your results indicate either a Core malfunction or deliberate interference with our measurement equipment."

The crowd parted as she approached, giving her a wide berth. Academy Masters commanded respect in the same way that storms commanded fear.

"You'll be admitted provisionally," she continued, "under the condition that you submit to weekly Core evaluations. If we discover any attempt to manipulate your readings, you'll be expelled and your family's reputation—" she glanced at his secondhand robes, "—such as it is, will be ruined."

Kael's Analytical Mind worked quickly. He could prove his Omega classification. The readings had been correct. But exposing the limitations of Thornwood's Core Reader would make enemies of every Master in the Academy.

"Understood," he said.

That night, Kael sat in his assigned dormitory—a small room in the eastern tower, shared with three other provisional students. His roommate, a pale boy named Theron whose Core radiated barely-contained fire magic, was already asleep.

But Kael wasn't sleeping. His Analytical Mind was running through the data from the examination, looking for the flaw in Thornwood's methodology.

There it was. The Core Reader measured mana *output*, not mana *potential*. A Core's true classification was determined by the ratio of accessible to inaccessible energy—something the machine couldn't detect. Omega-grade Cores would show as normal because their inaccessible reserves were, by definition, inaccessible to measurement.

Thornwood's equipment was measuring the wrong thing.

And Kael had proof.
