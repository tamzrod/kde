## 2. Research Question

Can KDE-ENGINE-003 (Gamma), through repeated independent causal synthesis, discover the semantic structure of mathematical expressions **without relying on prior mathematical knowledge or memorized equations**?

---

## 3. Objective

Determine whether KDE discovers mathematical semantics from the structure of expressions themselves rather than recalling previously learned mathematical concepts.

### What This Experiment IS
- Structural reasoning from mathematical form
- Discovery of semantic building blocks
- Causal relationship identification
- Ontology emergence through repeated analysis

### What This Experiment IS NOT
- Equation solving
- Famous formula identification
- Physics law recognition
- Knowledge retrieval

## 6. Major Findings

### Finding 1: Universal Ontology Emerged
**6 concepts** achieved 100% support across all 15 expressions:
- Dependency, Input-Output, Composition, Equivalence, Transformation, Aggregation

### Finding 2: Common Components Discovered
**5 concepts** achieved 70-80% support:
- Scaling, Ratio, Symmetry, Non-linearity, Iteration

### Finding 3: Ontology Stable
Discovery rate decreased from 7.0/expression (early) to 2.4/expression (late)
→ Indicates approaching saturation

### Finding 4: Finite Vocabulary
Mathematics can be represented with ~21 stable semantic concepts:
- 6 universal (core)
- 5 common (frequent)
- ~10 occasional (contextual)

### Finding 5: Symbol Independence
Ontology emerged regardless of variable names (Greek, Latin) or notation

### Finding 6: Prior Knowledge Not Required
No formula recognition - purely structural reasoning succeeded
