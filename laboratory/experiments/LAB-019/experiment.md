## RESEARCH QUESTION

Can KDE-ENGINE-003 (Gamma), through repeated independent causal synthesis, discover the semantic structure of mathematical expressions **without relying on prior mathematical knowledge or memorized equations**?

---

## OBJECTIVE

Determine whether KDE discovers mathematical semantics from the structure of expressions themselves rather than recalling previously learned mathematical concepts.

### What This Experiment IS

- Structural reasoning from mathematical form
- Discovery of semantic building blocks
- Causal relationship identification in expressions
- Ontology emergence through repeated analysis

### What This Experiment IS NOT

- Equation solving
- Famous formula identification

- Knowledge retrieval

## METHODOLOGY

### Per-Expression Analysis

For EACH expression, perform:

1. **Entity Identification**: Variables, constants, operators, functions, transformations
2. **Structural Relationships**: Inputs, outputs, dependencies, hierarchy, composition, nesting
3. **Causal Reasoning**: Why each component exists, influence patterns, invariants
4. **Semantic Discovery**: Dependency, balance, symmetry, conservation, transformation, etc.
5. **Confidence Assignment**: Confidence level, evidence, alternative interpretations

### Variable Run Strategy

- **Minimum Runs**: 10
- **Maximum Runs**: 100
- **Stop Conditions**:
  1. No new semantic concepts for 5 consecutive runs
  2. Ontology convergence > 95%
  3. Confidence stabilizes within ±2%
  4. Maximum runs reached

### Cross-Run Analysis

After every run, update:
- Semantic component frequency
- Newly discovered concepts
- Convergence percentage
- Discovery timeline
- Confidence trend

## SUCCESS CRITERIA

The experiment is successful if evidence supports ANY of:

2. **Multiple competing ontologies emerge** - Alternative interpretations
3. **No stable ontology exists** - Evidence for impossibility

All outcomes are scientifically valid.
