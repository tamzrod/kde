## 5. Major Findings

### Finding 1: Convergence on Root Cause
All three engines independently identified the same root cause: **upstream propellant feed system instability**.

**Evidence:** 100% agreement across 15 independent runs

### Finding 2: Confidence Sophistication
Engine maturity correlates with confidence sophistication:
- **Alpha:** Qualitative (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW)
- **Beta:** Statistical (88% ± 6%)
- **Gamma:** Causal (84% ± 8%)

### Finding 3: Explanation Depth
- **Alpha:** Correlation identification
- **Beta:** Context conditions and boundaries
- **Gamma:** Causal mechanisms and intervention predictions

### Finding 4: Engineering Value
| Engine | Value | Evidence |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Alpha | Moderate | Actionable but vague recommendations |
| Beta | High | Specific conditions documented |
| Gamma | Excellent | Prioritized intervention predictions |

### Finding 5: Reproducibility
All engines demonstrated **100% primary conclusion agreement** across all runs.

## 10. Conclusions

### Direct Observations

1. **All engines converge on same root cause** (100% agreement)
2. **Confidence expression differs** by engine maturity
3. **Engineering value increases** with engine sophistication
4. **Reproducibility is 100%** across all engines and runs
5. **No contradictions detected** in any run

### Interpretations

1. **Pattern engines** (Alpha) are suitable for initial exploration
2. **Context engines** (Beta) are suitable for production validation
3. **Causal engines** (Gamma) are suitable for root cause analysis and intervention planning
4. **Engine selection** should depend on use case, not overall score

### Cautions

1. **Single problem domain** - results may not generalize
2. **No ground truth** - cannot validate accuracy
3. **Human executor bias** - potential for unconscious influence
