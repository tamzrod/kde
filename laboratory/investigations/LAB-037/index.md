## Research Question

Can knowledge be decomposed into the smallest semantically complete primitive that can be independently stored, validated, referenced, and recombined into higher-level knowledge structures?

---

## Conclusion

**Multiple competing primitive models exist.**

No single knowledge primitive emerges as universally superior. The appropriate primitive depends on knowledge type, use case, validation requirements, and composition needs.

---

| Finding | Confidence | Description |

## Candidate Models

| Model | Best For | Properties |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Propositional | Facts, claims | High completeness, high independence |
| Contextual | Engineering | High completeness, low independence |
| Causal | Science | High completeness, low independence |
| Procedural | Engineering | High completeness, low independence |

---

## Recommended Approach

**Use contextual primitives as the primary model for KDE**, including:
- Claim (what is known)
- Context (when, where, for whom)
- Evidence (supporting documentation)
- Confidence (subjective probability)
- Relationships (links to other primitives)
