# Conclusion: LAB-038 Knowledge Taxonomy Investigation

**Investigation**: LAB-038
**Date**: 2026-07-22
**Status**: COMPLETE

---

## Summary

This investigation examined whether knowledge can be classified into a small number of fundamental categories. The investigation built upon LAB-037's finding that multiple competing primitive models exist, and sought to determine whether those models naturally belong to broader categories.

## Research Question

Can knowledge be classified into a small number of fundamental categories, each possessing its own semantically complete primitive?

## Evidence-Based Conclusion

**A defensible knowledge taxonomy exists.**

The evidence supports a six-factor taxonomy that can classify all knowledge objects, including KDE artifacts.

---

## The Six-Factor Knowledge Taxonomy

| Category | Symbol | Core Meaning | Example |
|----------|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Declarative** | D | "That" knowledge | "Water boils at 100°C" |
| **Procedural** | P | "How" knowledge | "How to boil water" |
| **Causal** | C | "Why" knowledge | "Why water boils at 100°C" |
| **Contextual** | X | "When/where/for whom" | "Valid at sea level" |
| **Evidential** | E | Support relationships | "This measurement supports..." |
| **Normative** | N | Obligations and rules | "Must maintain 100°C" |
