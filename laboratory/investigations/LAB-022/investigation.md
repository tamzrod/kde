## Research Question

> What is the fundamental architecture of a KDE Knowledge Document?

---

## Scope

### Included
- Definition of Knowledge Document identity
- Mandatory vs optional metadata
- Required vs optional sections
- Provenance preservation
- Revision management
- Knowledge lifecycle
- Relationships to KDE components
- Document class hierarchy

### Excluded
- Modifying the Knowledge Repository
- Implementing document templates
- Promoting knowledge

---

## Background

LAB-021 identified inconsistencies in the Knowledge Repository. This investigation determines what a Knowledge Document fundamentally is and should be, rather than describing current state.

---

## Investigation Areas

### 1. Identity
- What identifies a Knowledge Document?
- How is it named?
- What category does it belong to?

### 2. Metadata
- What metadata is mandatory?
- What metadata is optional?
- What metadata should never appear?

### 3. Content
- What sections are mandatory?
- What sections are optional?
- What content should never appear?

### 4. Provenance
- How does Knowledge trace back to Investigation?
- How is evidence preserved?
- How is approval recorded?

### 5. Lifecycle
- What states can Knowledge have?
- How does Knowledge change?
- How is obsolete Knowledge handled?

### 6. Relationships
- How does Knowledge relate to Laboratory?
- How does Knowledge relate to Governance?
- How does Knowledge relate to Engines?

### 7. Document Classes
- Should all Knowledge share one template?
- Are document classes required?
- What classes exist?

---

## Hypotheses (to be validated)

1. **H1**: Knowledge Documents are fundamentally different from Investigation artifacts
2. **H2**: A single universal template is insufficient
3. **H3**: Provenance is the most critical aspect
4. **H4**: Knowledge lifecycle differs from Investigation lifecycle
5. **H5**: Document classes map to KDE component categories
